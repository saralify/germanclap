Detailed: Why Germany is the best option to consider for your foreign education

Free Education No Tuition fees

Germany is the only top study destination where universities do not charge tuition fees, at a time when higher education is becoming a need owing to an increased demand for highly skilled workers and the cost of attending university is rising quicker.

All overseas students attending a public university in Germany would be exempt from paying standard tuition rates, according to a decision made in October 2014. Foreign students will only be responsible for a few administrative charges, such as a student contribution, a student union fee, and a public transportation ticket, totaling roughly €250 each semester.

Top Universities Research based Institutions

According to official records from 2018, Germany has 429 public higher education institutions, 106 of which are universities. Germany's universities are among the best in the world.

Some of them have constantly been named among the greatest colleges in the world. Students respect these colleges for their educational quality, hands-on experiences throughout their studies, opportunity to progress intellectually during and after graduation, and, most significantly, the safe and welcoming environment.

They have a global reputation for teaching and research quality, which stems from a long and illustrious history of higher education. Every year, a number of new German institutions are added to the worldwide rankings, in addition to the usual top-ranked universities.

Thousand Degree Options

Germany has a huge number of universities and provides a wide range of degree programmes to fit everyone's interests. Germany has made significant investments in engineering colleges as an industrialised country, and engineering majors are highly recognised in German universities today. However, these colleges provide a wide range of educational programmes, and some of them, such as medicine and pharmacy, are world leaders.

Furthermore, established courses in German universities are rapidly increasing as new study disciplines emerge in response to advances in science. Whether you want to learn about atoms or fascinating galaxies in the cosmos, Germany is certain to have a programme that will teach you about it.

Travel Europe Without Visa

The residence card also allows you to travel visa-free within the Schengen Area, which is a fantastic way to see the countries you want to see in a limited amount of time. It is recommended that you apply for a student visa at least 3 months before your anticipated travel date once you obtain a letter of admission from the specified German university (the time you are expected to be in Germany).

In terms of the resident permit, all of your documents will be forwarded to the Immigration Office of the German area where your university is located, and you will be awarded the residency permit once all of the bureaucratic procedures have been completed.

120 Days Work Permit For Students

International students are permitted to work part-time in Germany for up to 20 hours per week or 120 days per year, according to German legislation. More than 60% of international students now studying in Germany work part-time, according to estimates.

You don't even need a qualification because there are so many different professions to choose from that you'll most likely find anything. Working part-time while studying is a viable choice in Germany, even if you don't require additional funds to cover living expenses if your programme does not involve a placement year. Working experience may help you become more employable in the future, teach you how to be more disciplined in your life, and teach you how to live independently.

German Language Your new companion

here are several reasons why learning German is good to you, ranging from future employment to emotional bliss. Germany has one of the world's most strong economies, and German is the most widely spoken native language on the European continent. German corporations are worldwide leaders with a vast network of subsidiaries all over the globe.during and after graduation, and, most significantly, the safe and welcoming environment.

As a result, by speaking German, you are putting yourself in a situation where they will approach you and offer you a job. On the other side, the German language is widely spoken around the world, which means you will have the possibility to explore the globe and visit locations you have never been before.

Cost of Living Is Low

The cost of living in Germany for international students is relatively cheap with some careful money management and minor changes to your former lifestyle. Ordinarily, pricing in metropolitan locations would be greater than those in rural areas. Rent is the most significant financial worry you will face while studying in Germany. However, you have numerous possibilities, and while the cost of rent varies (mostly based on the location of the apartment), it is not too costly.

To reduce your financial load, find someone with whom you can share a room, which will lower your costs in half. Food, facilities, public transportation, and other products and services are not expensive.

Diverse Culture & Community

Other nations have long used Germany as a transit point. German citizens now coexist peacefully with many foreigners who have come to work and live with their families in Germany. Moreover, German institutions attract thousands of overseas students who see Germany as a way to achieve their educational objectives. In Germany, you will experience a varied population, each with its own unique characteristics that will cause you to see the world in a new light.

Germany's past is lengthy, rich, and complex, with relics strewn across the land. You probably know less about Germany today than you think. Throughout crucial periods of human history, the country has been a major actor. More about culture may be found in our in-depth German culture blog.

Bright Future Unlimited Opportunity

A degree from a German institution is a globally recognised and regarded credential. As a result, German graduates have a high rate of employability in the international labour market.

As a result, once you've completed your degree, a slew of firms will approach you with enticing job offers. They believe you can help them develop their business because of your professional credentials, which you earned via an excellent school in Germany. Graduates from German universities are among the best-paid employees since they are such in-demand job candidates. Regardless of your academic subject or the location in which you wish to work, a German degree can assist you in obtaining a well-paid and ideal position.
